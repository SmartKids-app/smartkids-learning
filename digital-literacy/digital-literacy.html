Topic One

What is Digital Literacy? 🖥️

Digital literacy means understanding how to use computers, phones, tablets, and the internet correctly, safely, and confidently for learning and daily life.

💻

Using Digital Devices

Digital literacy begins with knowing how to use computers, smartphones, tablets, printers, keyboards, and other digital tools around us.

🌍

Connecting to the World

With digital skills, students can search information online, attend classes, send messages, and learn from people across the world.

🧠

Thinking Smart

Digital literacy is not only pressing buttons. It also means knowing what is true, what is safe, and how to make wise decisions online.

🔒

Safety First

A digitally literate student protects passwords, avoids dangerous links, and understands how to stay safe while using the internet.

Simple Example

If a student can type an assignment, search for learning materials online, send it by email, and avoid fake websites — that student is practicing digital literacy.

Digital literacy helps school life, work life, and everyday life.

Lesson Summary

Digital literacy means using technology with knowledge, confidence, and responsibility. It helps students become smart learners and safe digital citizens.

“Use technology to grow, not to waste.”
