The Internet Guard

Not everything on the internet is what it seems. Learn how to spot fake websites, dangerous links, and keep your computer virus-free!

🔒 https://www.kidventor.com.ng

Look for the "S" 🛡️

Always check the address bar before you type anything. HTTPS means "Secure." If you only see "HTTP" or a red warning, the website might be eavesdropping on you!

✅

The Padlock means your data is encrypted (turned into a secret code).

Think Before You Click! 🖱️

Hackers use "Phishing" (pronounced 'fishing') to trick you into clicking bad links. They pretend to be your school, a game, or a friend.

The Bait 🎣

"Click here to get 1,000,000 free coins in your favorite game!"

The Shield 🛡️

Hover your mouse over the link first. If the address looks weird, DON'T CLICK!

Keep Your Computer Healthy 🩺

🚫

Unknown Downloads

Never download files or "mods" from sites you don't know. They could contain viruses.

🔄

Keep Updating

Updates are like medicine for your computer. They fix holes that hackers try to use.

🔌

Stranger USBs

Never plug a USB drive you found on the ground into your computer. It’s a common trick!

📶

Public Wi-Fi Danger

Open Wi-Fi at airports or cafes is like talking loudly in a crowded room—everyone can "hear" what your phone is doing. **Never log into bank accounts or school portals on public Wi-Fi.**
