Log Summary // Act 01

Two young couples overcome tribal barriers through the support of their fathers. While Adiza secures a job, Obalade supports the family business, proving that partnership matters more than ego.

The Foundation

Adiza and Obalade were a match the world said wouldn't work. She from the North, he from the Southwest. While their siblings worried about old tribal stories, the couple simply laughed the matters away.

After one year of marriage, they had no big house or luxury, but they had understanding. Adiza, a Business Admin graduate, got a job as a Store Manager. Obalade, an Agric Scientist and Computer Engineer, supported his mother at her vegetable stand.

At the store, the driver Kazim resigns suddenly for a bank job, leaving Adiza with heavy supplies and no driver. She takes the keys herself.
TINUKE (Staff)

Madam, you want to supply yourself? Ah! You even look more prettier, sexy in that uniform!

ADIZA

What will I do? Customers won't listen to excuses. Load the bigger bus, let me move. I won't sit watching my business go down.

On the road, she pulls over to pick up Obalade, who is looking for a bike to go buy computer parts.
ADIZA

(In Pidgin) Na so e be oo, Kazim resigned just this morning. Please come inside, where are you going?

OBALADE

(Laughing) Wao! You look stunning. Let me drive you instead. I can help you with this today; my repairs can wait until the weekend.

Log Summary // Act 02

Obalade joins the company as a driver to support Adiza's leadership. However, Adiza’s mother visits with traditional expectations, causing a clash between their modern logic and her old-school demands.

The Mother Error

ADIZA

You look just like my husband, Mr... Ah! You do exactly like my grandpa did for Grandma. He was such an amazing man. My dad supported my mum too, and I guess that support earned me my certificates.

OBALADE

My love, I can do this work. The owners are abroad; you have the right to employ who you like. I’ll be your boss at home, but you are my boss at work. I answer "Yes Ma'am" and deliver timely.

The staff begins to learn from their cooperation. But soon, Adiza’s mother visits. She is shocked to see Obalade in a driver's uniform and begins a rebuke in Hausa.
MUM ADIZA

Where are my grandchildren? Sir! Sit there! So you cannot get a woman pregnant? You tell your father she is pregnant just so I can come and see her!

A phone call from Adiza's Father reveals that the Mother lied about the pregnancy just to visit Lagos for four days without being questioned.
ADIZA

Mum, Dad thinks I am pregnant because of you! You lied just to visit your friends for four days? Dad misses you in Abuja. Please, go back to him. We are happy and my mother-in-law is caring.
