The Tortoise and the Birds' Feast

Reading Time: 5 mins • Category: Igbo Folklore

There was a great famine in the animal kingdom, but the Birds were invited to a feast in the sky by the Sky People.

The Tortoise, who was very hungry and clever, convinced the Birds to take him along. Since he had no wings, each bird gave him one feather to make a pair of wings for him. Before they reached the sky, Tortoise suggested they all take new names for the feast. He chose the name "All of You."

"He who wants everything often ends up with nothing."

When the Sky People served the delicious food, they said, "This is for all of you." Tortoise stepped forward and ate almost everything, claiming the food was specifically for him! The Birds were left with only scraps.

Angry at his greed, the Birds took back their feathers. Tortoise had no wings to fly home and had to jump from the sky. He crashed onto the hard ground, and that—the elders say—is why the Tortoise's shell is cracked and bumpy today.

💡 The Lesson Learned

Greed leads to isolation. In the digital world, "greed" can look like hoarding information or taking all the credit for a group project. When we share our knowledge (like teaching a friend how to use a software), the whole "village" grows stronger.

Quick Reflection:

Why did the Birds take back their feathers?
