1971: The First Stamp

Before the web, Ray Tomlinson sent the first network email. He chose the @ symbol to separate the user's name from their computer's name. It was like magic—sending mail without a truck!

"I sent the message to myself, from one machine to another." — Ray T.

Who can have an account?

Almost anyone! Most sites need you to be 13, but schools provide student boxes. Every address has 3 parts:

kid_hero@gmail.com
Username: Your unique digital name.
Domain: The "Post Office" hosting your mail.

Gmail Training

Gmail is the world's most popular post office. Here’s what’s inside:

  • 📥 Inbox: Where your new mail lands.
  • ➕ Compose: Click this to write a new letter.
  • 📎 Attachment: The paperclip icon to send photos/files.

Defeating Spam & Phishing

Email is amazing, but some people use it to be "Digital Pirates."

🥫 SPAM

Digital junk mail. Annoying, but harmless. Just delete it!

🎣 PHISHING

Fake mail trying to steal your password. NEVER click their links.
