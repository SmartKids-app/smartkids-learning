Module 2: Connectivity

How the Internet Works

The "Magic" of how computers talk to each other across the world.

1. A Giant Web of Cables

Many people think the Internet is in the clouds, but it is actually a giant network of physical cables that run under the ground and across the bottom of the ocean!

When you send a message from Nigeria to a friend in another country, your data travels through these cables at the speed of light.

📮 The Digital Post Office

Think of the Internet like a Post Office. Every computer has a unique address called an IP Address (like your home address).

  • 1. The Letter: Your email or photo is broken into small pieces called "Packets."
  • 2. The Envelope: Each packet has the destination address written on it.
  • 3. The Router: Routers are like mail trucks that move your packets from one network to another until they reach the destination.

The Server

A powerful computer that "serves" information. When you go to google.com, your computer asks Google's server to send it the website.

The Browser

Apps like Chrome or Safari that act as your "window" to view the information sent by servers.
